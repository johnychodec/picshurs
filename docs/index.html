Why Picshurs?

Built for photographers, family albums, and anyone who wants their media to stay local.

☁️

No Cloud Required

Keep your photos and videos on your own disk. No forced upload, no account creation, no lock-in.

🔒

Private by Default

Browsing, indexing, OCR, and face detection run locally with Apple Vision. Your data never leaves your Mac.

📂

Folder-First Workflow

Use your existing folders directly. No import step, no proprietary library — just watch and browse.

🎨

Non-Destructive Editing

Crop, straighten, adjust, and apply 28 filters to photos — originals are never touched. Edits live in tiny JSON sidecars.

⚡

Built for Large Libraries

Designed for 10,000+ item libraries. SQLite-backed grid renders in milliseconds with background filesystem scanning.

⌨️

Keyboard Driven

Nearly every action has a shortcut. Tag, pin, export, and navigate without leaving the keyboard.

Features

Everything you need to organize mixed photo and video libraries.

🏷️

Smart Tags & Search

8-color dot tags, full-text filename search, OCR text-in-photo search, and a Show videos toggle.

🗺️

Map View

Geotagged photos plotted on an interactive MapKit map. Explore memories by location.

👤

People (Experimental)

On-device face detection and clustering. Name, merge, and hide people — all locally.

🖼️

Mixed Gallery

Videos sit beside photos with poster thumbnails and play badges. Click the poster to open your default video player.

🔄

50-Level Undo

Per-photo undo/redo with stackable adjustment layers in any order you choose.

📋

The Tray

Pin items to a persistent working set, reorder, and batch-export or move the entire selection.

What makes Picshurs different

Native macOS Experience

SwiftUI, Core Image GPU pipeline, MapKit, Quick Look — not a webview wrapper. It feels like it belongs on your Mac.

True Non-Destructive Edits

Unlike organizers that only crop and rotate, Picshurs gives photos adjustment layers, filters, and full undo — without ever touching originals.

No Model Downloads

AI features use Apple Vision framework on-device. Zero setup, no ONNX blobs, no waiting — it just works.
